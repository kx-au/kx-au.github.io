Au Khai Xiang

PhD student in Integrative Sciences (Statistics)
National University of Singapore

I have worked on Markov chain Monte Carlo methods for efficient Bayesian inference under the supervision of Alexandre Thiery and Matt Graham. Before that, I have completed a B.Eng. (Hons) in Engineering Science at NUS with a specialisation in Computational Science. I am awaiting my PhD oral defense and I am currently open to job opportunities (CV here).

Research interests: Hamiltonian Monte Carlo methods, uncertainty quantification, Bayesian inverse problems, Bayesian deep learning, computational physics and finance.

Livestreaming

To motivate myself to be more productive under pandemic times, since July 2021 I (try to) regularly stream my work on Twitch and can be found on Youtube, where I go by the username Columnspaces. I hope for laymen to find it interesting to see what my research looks like, and for researchers to seek comfort in watching me struggle. Here is me learning about Delayed-Rejection MCMC and taking notes as a new LyX user.

Pandemic
I know nothing about epidemiology so I read the minimum and apply some common sense to simulate disease spread, e.g. COVID19. I allow re-infection with some low probability.
